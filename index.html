Showdown

Best 5-card hand wins the pot
▼

Remaining players reveal their hole cards. Best 5-card hand from 2 hole cards + 5 community cards wins. You can use both, one, or none of your hole cards.

Stealing the Blinds

CO / BTN open-raise when folded to you
▼

When action folds to you in the Cutoff or Button, only the blinds remain. Raising forces them to defend or fold — either outcome is profitable for you.

Why it works

The SB acts before you post-flop. The BB has already invested 1BB so feels pressure to protect it. Both players' ranges are capped — they didn't raise pre-flop, so they rarely have monsters.

DoRaise 2.2–2.5x from BTN with 40–50% of all hands. Mix in suited connectors, Axs, any two broadways.
Don'tLimp in. Limping gives the BB a free look and kills your ability to win the pot before the flop.

Defending the Big Blind

How to respond to pre-flop raises
▼

You've already posted 1BB — this gives you pot odds to defend wider than any other position. Know when to call, 3-bet, or fold based on who raised and from where.

Why it works

If someone raises 2.5x from the BTN you're getting roughly 3:1 odds to call — you only need to win ~25% of the time to break even. That justifies calling with many hands you'd fold from other positions.

DoCall wider vs BTN steals. 3-bet your premium hands and balanced bluff candidates like low suited connectors.
Don'tDefend vs UTG opens with weak hands — their range is far stronger. Fold more, not less, vs early position.

Continuation Betting

Betting the flop after raising pre-flop
▼

As the pre-flop raiser you have range advantage — your range is generally stronger. A c-bet continues the story of strength, whether or not you connected with the board.

Why it works

The flop misses most players most of the time. When you bet, your opponent must decide if you hit. If they also missed they'll often fold rather than face further bets on the turn.

C-bet whenFlop is dry (e.g. K♠ 7♥ 2♦) and favours your raising range. In position. Heads-up pot.
Check back whenFlop is wet / connected (e.g. 8♥ 9♥ T♦). Multi-way pot. You missed and have no draw.

Facing a 3-Bet

Someone re-raises your open — what now?
▼

You opened the pot and someone 3-bet you. You now face three choices: fold, call, or 4-bet. The right answer depends on your hand, position, and the 3-bettor's tendencies.

Why it works

Most players 3-bet infrequently — meaning their range when they do is very strong. When someone 3-bets, give them credit. Don't call off your stack with TT just because you "feel" they're bluffing.

4-Bet withAA, KK, QQ, AK for value. Also balanced bluffs: AJs, KQs that you'd fold anyway — makes your range hard to read.
Don'tCall 3-bets out of position with marginal hands like KJo, QTo. You'll be playing large pots from a disadvantaged spot every time.

Value Betting

Extracting maximum chips from strong hands
▼

A value bet is a bet you make wanting to be called — because you believe you have the best hand. The goal is to build the pot, not protect it.

Why it works

Players who miss value bets leave enormous money on the table over time. If your opponent calls a 75% pot bet with worse, betting 30% "for safety" is a massive leak — you're choosing a smaller payout for no reason.

DoBet large (60–90% pot) when your hand is very strong and the board is dry. Ask: "what hands worse than mine will call?"
Don'tSlow play by default. Checking sets and flushes "to trap" gives opponents free cards to outdraw you and kills the pot size.

Set Mining

Calling pre-flop with small pocket pairs
▼

Set mining is calling a pre-flop raise with a small pocket pair (22–77) aiming to hit three-of-a-kind on the flop. Sets are disguised and win massive pots.

Why it works

You'll hit a set roughly 1 in 8 times (12%). That sounds low, but sets are so strong and well-disguised that when you hit, you often stack your opponent. The math works when stacks are deep enough.

Good spotsEffective stacks are 15x+ the raise size. Against one raiser who can pay you off. In position or multi-way.
Bad spotsShort-stacked games (less than 15x the call). When raiser is tight and folds to your flop bet if they miss.

Playing Draws

Flush draws, straight draws — call or semi-bluff?
▼

A draw needs one more card to complete (e.g. four to a flush). A flush draw on the flop has ~35% equity. You can call for pot odds, or semi-bluff raise to win two ways.

Why it works

Semi-bluffing with a draw is powerful because you can win by fold equity now OR by hitting your draw. You don't need it to work every time — just often enough given your equity when called.

Semi-bluff whenIn position. Board favours your range. Strong draw (flush + straight combo = ~54% equity).
Don'tChase draws without pot odds. Don't call multiple streets with a gutshot (4 outs, ~9% to hit).

Positional Awareness

Adjusting your game based on where you sit
▼

Position is the single most important strategic concept in poker. Acting last means you see what everyone does before deciding. This advantage compounds across every street.

Why it works

In position you can call more, bluff more accurately, value bet thinner, and control pot size. Out of position you're guessing. Same hand, different seat = completely different profitability.

In positionWiden your range. Float more. Extract extra value. Control pot size with medium-strength hands.
Out of positionTighten up. Don't bluff multi-street without a clear plan. Use check-raise to regain initiative.
